> Chapter 1: What is a 3D Model?

This is the content for Subchapter 1.

A 3D model is a digital representation of an object in three dimensions — width, height, and depth. It’s made of a mesh — tiny triangles stitched together to define the object’s shape. These files usually come in formats like .STL (the most common format for 3D printing), .OBJ (supports textures and color data ), or .3MF (designed specifically for 3D printing with more detailed metadata).
